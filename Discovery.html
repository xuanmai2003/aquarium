FAQS

When is the Discovery Dive program offered?

Stay tuned for new days and times.

Do I need to provide you with proof of my dive certification?

Yes, we require, in advance, proof of your S.C.U.B.A certification (e.g. photo of card-front and back, screen shot of PADI app or webpage). You must also bring the certification document with you on the day of your dive.

What is the cost of the dive program?

The program cost is $350 plus tax for the first guest, and a second guest can be added to the same session for an additional $200 plus tax. Ripleyâs Aquarium of Canada is proud to donate 10% of proceeds from the Discovery Dive program to shark conservation projects.

How many people can participate at a time?

The program is limited to two (2) guests at a time.

What are the age requirements?

All participants must be 16 years and older. Guests under the age of 18 must be accompanied by a participating adult in the water.

What is included in the Program?

The dive program includes admission to the Aquarium, a Behind the Scenes tour, 30 minute dive in the Dangerous Lagoon Exhibit, and souvenir items to take home (towel, bag, photographs of your experience on a USB flash drive).

Will annual pass holders receive a discount?

Yes, annual pass holders receive a 10% discount.

Can I use my own dive equipment?

In order to ensure the safety of our animals and avoid cross contamination Ripleyâs Aquarium of Canada will supply all equipment (fins, bcd, regulator, weights, wetsuit -3mm and booties). Personal masks are recommended if you have one, but RAC will provide one if you donât.

What else do I need to bring?

It is mandatory to bring and wear a bathing suit under the dive suit. You are welcome to bring any personal toiletries you wish for after the dive, however shampoo, conditioner, and body wash are provided. You may want to bring flip flops to wear in the locker room.

Can I bring my own go-pro or underwater camera?

Only Aquarium husbandry staff members are permitted to feed our animals.

Where do I store my belongings?

There is a guest locker room where you will be able to store your belongings, change and shower.

How much time do we need to experience the whole dive program?

The dive program lasts for approximately 2 hours from start to finish and includes a 30 minute guided S.C.U.B.A. dive in the Dangerous Lagoon Exhibit.

How big is the Dangerous Lagoon Exhibit?

The exhibit is 750,000 gallons (2.8 million liters) and is 12 feet (4 meters) deep.

Is there parking at the aquarium?

You can reserve your parking spot PRIOR to visiting the aquarium using the following link. View real time availability and pricing at convenient locations within walking distance to Ripleyâs Aquarium of Canada. Once booked, your spot is 100% guaranteed at the selected location.

What animals can I expect to see in the dive?

This exhibit is home to sand tiger sharks, sandbar sharks, nurse sharks, green sawfish, roughtail stingrays, green sea turtles and many species of tropical western Atlantic fishes.

Do I have to book in advance?

Yes. In order to guarantee your booking you purchase your Discovery Dive ticket in advance, and provide your sizing information, proof of dive certification and medial waiver in advance.

Do you require any medical information?

Yes, you will be asked to provide a medical certificate prior to your dive. The medical form can be downloaded from our site. Please review the medical form closely. If you answer yes to any of the questions the form must be signed by a physician before coming for your dive. We reserve the right to cancel any dive if paperwork is incomplete.

Do I need proof of my last dive?

Yes, we require your last dive to be within the past two (2) years. Provide a copy of your log book as proof.

What if I haven't been diving the past two years?

We require that you participate in a refresher course (offered through many dive shops) and provide proof of completion prior to your dive.

Do I need to be able to speak English to participate?

Yes, all divers must have basic proficiency in spoken English.

Do I need to sign a liability waiver?

All participants must sign a liability waiver. Participants under the age of 18 must have the liability waivers signed and dated by their parent or court appointed legal guardian.

Are you able to dive with special needs?

Unfortunately our exhibit entrance is not designed to meet special needs at this time.

Can my size be accommodated?

We have a wide variety of gear sizes that can accommodate someone who weighs up to 320 lbs.

Are guests able to watch? Do they have to buy a ticket?

Yes, family and friends can watch the dive portion of the experience however they must purchase general admission tickets.

Where do I go when I arrive?

Proceed to the Guest Services desk to collect your tickets and they will direct you to your meeting location.

Will there be a guide in the water with me when I dive?

There will be two divers in the water with you. One diver will be an instructor or a dive master and the other will be a shark tender. There are also two divers on the dry side, one at the top to tend the dive and another to supervise the dive from the tunnel. Dive team members are CPR, First Aid, and emergency oxygen trained.

Do we have any free dive time in the tank?

The dive is a guided tour, not free swim.

What is the temperature of the water?

22 degrees Celsius

What is the thickness of the wetsuits?

Our wetsuits are 3mm.

Are hoods provided?

Hoods are not permitted as the guests must to be able to hear audible sounds from the shark tender.
